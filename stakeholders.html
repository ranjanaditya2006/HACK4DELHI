Global comparative context
Selected international experiences with synchronized or consolidated elections.
South Africa
National and provincial elections are synchronized, improving administrative efficiency. However, local elections remain separate, preserving subnational political competition.
Sweden
Fully synchronized elections across levels increased turnout stability, but required strong party institutionalization and high voter literacy to manage ballot complexity.
Philippines
Partial synchronization reduced election frequency but amplified campaign costs and elite dominance, raising concerns about unequal political access.
